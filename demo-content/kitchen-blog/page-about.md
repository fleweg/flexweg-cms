# About this blog

Kitchen Stories is a personal blog about home cooking. It's written by Anna Bianchi, who has been feeding people in her own kitchen for over fifteen years, mostly without a formal recipe to consult.

## Who writes here

Anna is a software developer based in Milan. By day she writes code; by evening, weekend, and the occasional weekday lunch she cooks. She trained briefly in a Roman trattoria as a teenager — long enough to learn how to make pasta but not long enough to consider it a career.

The blog started in 2024 as a place to save recipes she kept losing on scraps of paper. It grew because friends kept asking for the recipes.

## What you'll find

Practical recipes for home cooking. Technique notes where they matter. The occasional opinion about why one method works better than another.

This is **not** a competitive food blog. There are no twenty-step recipes that take three hours to read past the headnote. There are no affiliate links. There are no sponsored posts. The blog has run on a single Flexweg CMS site since launch, no ads, no tracking.

## Editorial policy

- Recipes are tested in Anna's own kitchen with the kind of equipment most home cooks have
- Posts may include links to specific products only when there's no easy substitute — and never as paid placements
- Comments are closed (spam is exhausting); the contact page is open for emails
- Corrections welcome — if a recipe doesn't work for you, the contact page tells you how to reach me

## Thank you

Thanks for reading. Cooking is one of the most useful things you can spend time on. If a recipe here saves you from a bad takeaway one Tuesday night, it's been worth writing.
