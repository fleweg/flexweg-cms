Hello. I'm Anna, and I cook a lot. Not professionally, not on television, just at home — for the people I live with and the friends who turn up on Sundays expecting something hot from the oven.

This blog is where I write down what's worked. Some of it is recipes I've been making for years. Some of it is methods I learned the hard way (mostly involving smoke, splattered grease, or chicken that wasn't quite done in the middle). All of it is the kind of thing I'd tell you over coffee if you asked.

## What you'll find here

- **Recipes** — the dishes that turn up on my table most often, written so you can actually follow them
- **Techniques** — knife skills, sourdough, the things worth practicing
- **Baking** — bread, cake, pizza dough
- **Lifestyle** — the small habits that make a home kitchen workable

Nothing here is restaurant cuisine. I don't own a sous-vide setup. My oven runs ten degrees hot. I cook with what's in the fridge and what's in season, and so does everyone I share recipes with.

## Why bother writing it down

Because I keep losing my own recipes. Because friends keep asking for them. Because I think home cooking is one of the best things you can spend time on, and the internet doesn't have enough of it written by people who actually eat the food they post about.

If something here doesn't work for you — wrong oven, missing ingredient, your tap water is hard — tell me. There's a contact page. I write back.

Let's start cooking.
