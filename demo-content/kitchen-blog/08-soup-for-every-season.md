Soup is the most forgiving thing in the kitchen. It uses up vegetables that are past their best, scales endlessly, freezes well, and feels like cooking even when you barely do anything. Here are three I make on rotation through the year.

## The method (works for all three)

1. Soften aromatics (onion, garlic, sometimes carrot/celery) slowly in olive oil
2. Add the main ingredient(s) and any spices
3. Add stock or water to just cover
4. Simmer until tender
5. Blend (or leave chunky), check seasoning, finish with something bright

That's it. Every soup below uses this same pattern.

## Winter: Tomato and white bean

Best for cold weeks when you want something warming and substantial.

**Ingredients (4 portions)**
- 2 tbsp olive oil
- 1 onion, diced
- 4 cloves garlic, minced
- 1 tbsp tomato paste
- 1 tsp smoked paprika
- 1 tin (400 g) chopped tomatoes
- 2 tins (400 g each) white beans (cannellini or butter beans), drained
- 800 ml chicken or vegetable stock
- A parmesan rind if you have one (worth saving for soup)
- Salt, black pepper
- A drizzle of good olive oil + chopped parsley to finish

**Method:** Soften onion 8 minutes. Add garlic, paprika, tomato paste — cook 1 minute. Add tomatoes, beans, stock, parmesan rind. Simmer 25 minutes. Fish out the rind. Mash a third of the beans against the side of the pot for body. Season. Finish.

## Spring: Pea and mint

Best when fresh peas come back into the markets — frozen work perfectly the rest of the year.

**Ingredients (4 portions)**
- 2 tbsp butter
- 1 leek (white and pale green only), sliced
- 600 g peas (fresh or frozen)
- 800 ml vegetable stock
- A handful of fresh mint leaves
- Juice of half a lemon
- Salt, black pepper
- Crème fraîche to swirl in, extra mint for garnish

**Method:** Sweat the leek in butter, low heat, 10 minutes — don't brown. Add peas and stock. Bring to a simmer, cook 5 minutes (3 if frozen). Off the heat: add mint leaves and lemon juice. Blend until very smooth. Season. Serve hot with a swirl of crème fraîche.

## Summer: Gazpacho

The one soup that's served cold. Best made the day before so the flavors marry.

**Ingredients (4 portions)**
- 1 kg ripe tomatoes (this matters — use the best ones you can find), cored
- 1 cucumber, peeled
- 1 red pepper, seeded
- Half a small red onion
- 2 cloves garlic
- 4 tbsp good olive oil
- 2 tbsp sherry vinegar
- A slice of stale white bread, soaked in water then squeezed out
- Salt
- Garnish: diced cucumber, diced pepper, croutons, basil leaves, more olive oil

**Method:** Blend everything in a high-speed blender until completely smooth. Push through a sieve if you want it silky (worth doing). Chill at least 4 hours, ideally overnight. Serve cold, in chilled bowls, with the garnishes scattered on top.

## Tips that apply to all three

- **Salt at the end.** Soups concentrate as they cook, and pre-seasoning can lead to over-salting.
- **Finish bright.** A squeeze of lemon, a drizzle of fresh oil, a handful of herbs — every soup is improved by something added at the last minute.
- **Freeze in portions.** Soup keeps 3 days in the fridge and 3 months in the freezer. Use sturdy zip-top bags laid flat so they freeze in thin slabs and thaw quickly.
- **Make stock once a month.** Even bad stock from a supermarket carton is better than water. Best is the homemade kind from roast chicken carcasses (see the roast chicken post above).

That's three soups for three seasons. The fourth — autumn — usually means roasted squash and apple, but that's a longer post.
