This is the cake I make for birthdays. Three layers, dark chocolate ganache between each, dusted with cocoa on top. It takes the better part of an afternoon but it keeps for four days, freezes well, and the recipe never fails me.

## Ingredients

### For the cake (makes three 20 cm / 8 inch layers)

- 350 g plain flour
- 90 g unsweetened cocoa powder (Dutch-processed)
- 400 g granulated sugar
- 1 tsp baking powder
- 2 tsp baking soda
- 1.5 tsp fine salt
- 4 large eggs
- 240 ml buttermilk
- 240 ml strong brewed coffee (cooled — yes, coffee, you won't taste it, it just deepens the chocolate)
- 180 ml neutral oil (sunflower, grapeseed)
- 2 tsp vanilla extract

### For the ganache

- 400 g good dark chocolate (60–70% cocoa), chopped
- 400 ml double cream
- A pinch of salt

## Method

### The cake

**1. Heat the oven to 175°C / 350°F.** Line the bottom of three 20 cm cake tins with baking parchment. Lightly butter the sides.

**2. Sift the dry ingredients together.** Flour, cocoa, sugar, baking powder, baking soda, salt. Whisk to combine in a large bowl.

**3. Mix the wet ingredients.** In another bowl: eggs, buttermilk, coffee, oil, vanilla. Whisk until smooth.

**4. Combine.** Pour wet into dry. Whisk until just combined — don't overmix. The batter will look thin. That's correct.

**5. Divide.** Pour evenly between the three tins (a scale is helpful here for layers that match).

**6. Bake for 28–32 minutes.** A skewer should come out with a few moist crumbs (not wet batter, not completely clean — somewhere in between).

**7. Cool.** Ten minutes in the tins, then turn out onto a rack. Let them cool completely before frosting. This takes longer than you think — give them at least 90 minutes. A warm layer melts the ganache.

### The ganache

**1. Chop the chocolate.** Small pieces, even-sized. Tip into a heat-safe bowl.

**2. Heat the cream.** Until it's just starting to steam — not boiling. Pour over the chocolate.

**3. Wait 2 minutes.** Don't stir yet. Let the heat melt the chocolate.

**4. Stir slowly from the centre out.** Until you have a glossy, smooth ganache. Add the pinch of salt.

**5. Cool to spreadable.** About 30 minutes at room temperature, stirring occasionally. It should be the texture of soft peanut butter when you spread it.

### Assembly

Place the first layer on your serving plate. Spread a third of the ganache. Top with the second layer. Repeat. Finish with ganache on top, swirled with the back of a spoon. Optional: dust with extra cocoa powder.

## A few notes

- **Don't substitute the buttermilk.** If you don't have any, mix 240 ml whole milk with a tablespoon of lemon juice and let it sit for 5 minutes. It curdles into instant buttermilk.
- **Coffee:** decaf works. Instant coffee dissolved in water works. The point is the flavor compound, not the caffeine.
- **Storage:** keeps 4 days at room temperature under a cake dome. Don't refrigerate — the cake dries out and the ganache gets too firm.
- **Make ahead:** the layers can be baked the day before and wrapped in cling film. Ganache can be made the day before too — just warm it gently before frosting.

## When to make this

For birthdays. For very bad weeks. For the kind of Sunday afternoon where you have nowhere else to be and the radio plays something nice. Some recipes are about the destination; this one is about the afternoon spent making it.
