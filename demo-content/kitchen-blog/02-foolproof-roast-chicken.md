If you only ever learn one dinner recipe, make it this one. A roast chicken feeds four people, takes about ninety minutes total, and produces enough leftovers for a soup the next day. There is no plant-based substitute for the way a properly-roasted bird smells coming out of the oven on a Sunday.

## What you need

- One whole chicken, about 1.6 to 1.8 kg / 3.5–4 lb (free-range if you can swing it — the difference is real)
- Two tablespoons soft butter
- One whole lemon, halved
- A few sprigs of thyme or rosemary
- Three cloves of garlic, whole, peeled
- Sea salt, black pepper
- Optional: a head of garlic cut in half, a few small potatoes for the pan

## Method

**1. Salt early.** This is the only step that really matters. Salt the chicken inside and out — properly, generously, like you mean it — at least one hour before roasting, ideally the day before. Leave it uncovered in the fridge. The skin dries out, which is what makes it crisp later.

**2. Bring to room temperature.** Take the bird out of the fridge an hour before it goes in the oven. A cold chicken cooks unevenly.

**3. Heat the oven to 220°C / 425°F.**

**4. Stuff and rub.** Pat the chicken dry. Stuff the cavity with the lemon halves, herbs, and garlic cloves. Rub butter all over the skin, especially the breast. Cross the legs and tie with kitchen twine if you like (you don't have to).

**5. Roast.** Place the bird breast-up on a roasting tray. Roast for about 1 hour 10 minutes for a 1.6 kg bird, 1 hour 20 for a 1.8 kg bird. The internal temperature at the thickest part of the thigh should read 74°C / 165°F. The juices should run clear.

**6. Rest.** Take the bird out, tent loosely with foil, and let it rest for 15 minutes before carving. This is non-negotiable — the meat needs to settle or all the juice ends up on your cutting board.

## Sides

Roasted potatoes (cut small, started in cold salted water, boiled 10 minutes, then roasted on the same tray for the last 40 minutes of the chicken). A simple green salad. A piece of bread to mop up the pan juices.

## What to do with the leftovers

Pull the meat from the carcass. Make stock with the bones (12 hours in a slow cooker with onion, carrot, celery). Eat the meat cold on toast with mayonnaise the next day. Don't throw the bones out.
